[I2V Prompt Rules]
Since a reference image is provided, the video model already knows:
- Character appearance, outfit, colors, materials
- Base lighting and color palette
- Environment visible in the frame

Your prompt must focus on what the video model CANNOT infer from a still image:

1. **Camera motion** — Static? Slow push-in? Pan? Track? How much movement over the shot duration?
2. **Subject animation** — What does the character DO? Describe the sequence of body movements. Be specific about bipedal posture, paw/hand actions, head movements, facial micro-expressions
3. **Physics & interaction** — How do objects respond? Steam rising, liquid pouring, fabric swaying, leaves falling
4. **Temporal sequence** — What happens FIRST, THEN, FINALLY across the 3-5 second clip
5. **Atmosphere cues** — Ambient motion in the environment (background people, wind, light changes)
6. **Audio-visual anchors** — Describe sounds as visible sources (mouth opens for dialogue, pan sizzles, door creaks)

[What to AVOID in I2V prompts]
- Do NOT describe the character's appearance, breed, fur color, or outfit — the image provides this
- Do NOT over-specify color grading or lighting setup — the image establishes the palette
- Do NOT use technical camera specs (f/1.8, 85mm, 3500K) — describe the FEELING instead
- Do NOT fight the reference image — enhance and animate what's already there

[Prompt Structure]
Write 80-150 words per shot as a cohesive paragraph:
- Open with camera behavior (1 sentence)
- Describe the action sequence (2-3 sentences) 
- Close with atmosphere/ambient motion (1 sentence)

Apply character animation rules and visual constraints from the Guide.
